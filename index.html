About Me

Why Web ?

Hello, You can call me Feng Li or just Feng . I know right, my name is pretty weird ,But the mean of it is prosperity and balance. So, I am a vocational high school student majoring in computer network engineering. Then why did I end up in the web field,which is usually pursued by software engineering majors? I'm also confused the initial reason was because of a student competency competition held at both the provincial and national levels. My teacher and seniors offered me two fields: Information Technology Software Solutions for Business and Web Technology.To be honest, I wanted to choose ITSSB because I thought it involved making game applications (even though it actually involves business applications) that I usually play on my phone. However, they told me to take Web Technology instead, as it is more exciting and includes games, as it is more exciting and has games. September 2024 was the first time I touched HTML. Everything was fun until I started learning JavaScript and PHP. That’s when I realized it wasn't as enjoyable as they said💀.

Because I had already committed, I forced myself to study it even though my logic didn't connect until around January 2025. That's when I started to wonder if I could pursue this field. However, when I began to delve deeper into the Laravel tech stack, I started to feel alive again as this was the best part of myself in this field, becoming a backend developer. After that, I learned React, and as I expected, frontend development was too difficult for me. One month before the competition, I studied tirelessly because I didn’t want to waste the months I had spent. Four days before the competition, the exam modules were almost 180 degrees different from what I had practiced, but I still tried to stay positive thinking that it was just a guideline and not the actual exam. That was my biggest mistake. On Day 1 of the competition, the modules used were from the guidelines; my face went pale and weak when I saw those modules. I did my best with the abilities I had.

The second module fortunately I could work on with 100% specification, and after that went home to prepare for the third module tomorrow, because for both previous modules according to my study guide I also studied the third module, I studied hard all night, I focused on the backend part because that is what I am best at, for the frontend I leave it to God Almighty 😁. And the next day I managed to work on 75% of the backend because the competition did not provide any collections or databases at all 😊, a bit shocking because usually they are already available. The frontend part, as I had expected, only included the login and register pages, but I should have been able to do more because the competition time suddenly was 3 hours, even though we were informed yesterday that it would be 6 hours, I don’t know why it was like that, and on the night of the announcement I was very nervous. Win or die was on my mind at that time, and with God's grace and all my efforts, I finally managed to win a gold medal. That is the end of the story.
